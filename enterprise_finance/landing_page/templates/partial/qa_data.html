Find answers to the most common questions about our Payroll & Compensation Suite.

How the system work?

The system starts by capturing company and employee financial data, including earnings and deductions. Users select a payroll month and define payroll components, then initiate payroll processing for that period. Adjustments can be made for errors or updates—such as missing earnings or incorrect deductions—by linking original and current payroll periods. The system calculates gross pay, tax, pension, and net pay automatically and updates adjusted and recorded values accordingly. Reports, journal entries, and expense summaries are generated to ensure accuracy, transparency, and traceability.

How Regular Payroll Work?

Enter employee financial information including earnings and deductions. Select the payroll month and relevant components, then go to “Make Payroll by Month” and choose the month to process. Add or update earnings and deductions as needed—multiple personnel can be processed in the same month. The system automatically calculates gross pay, tax, pension contributions, and net pay. Comprehensive reports, journal entries, and expense summaries are auto-generated for transparency and compliance.

How does adjustment work?

An adjustment is made when an error or update—such as a missing earning, incorrect deduction, or when additional earning or deduction is needed—is discovered after the original payroll period. The user selects the payroll component to adjust and the reason for the adjustment (e.g., underpayment, overpayment, correction). They then link the original payroll needing correction (payroll_needing_adjustment) to the affected period and assign the current payroll (record_month) where the adjustment will be recorded. After entering the adjustment amount, the system automatically calculates the taxable and non-taxable portions, updates the corrected values for the original period (adjusted_month_* fields), and updates the totals in the current period (recorded_month_* fields), including tax, pension, and expense figures.
